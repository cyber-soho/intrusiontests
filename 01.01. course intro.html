Ethics & Law

This section breaks down the three foundational pillars that differentiate ethical hacking from malicious intrusion. Understanding authorization, global legal frameworks, and professional codes of conduct is absolutely mandatory before any active testing begins.

📝

1. Authorization

Written permission is non-negotiable. Verbal agreements offer no protection.

  • Requires SoW or LoA.
  • Must be signed by the asset owner.
  • Must name specific targets.
⚠

2. Legal Compliance

Rule of Thumb: If unsure of legality in a jurisdiction, do not act.

  • US: CFAA
  • EU: NIS2, GDPR
  • Canada: Crim Code § 342.1, PIPEDA
👤

3. Professional Conduct

Adherence to industry codes protects your reputation and certification.

  • EC-Council / SANS / PTES Codes.
  • Violations lead to termination or criminal liability.

The Golden Rule

The human is accountable — not the model. Every AI-generated output must be reviewed, validated, and approved by a qualified tester.

  • Hallucinated CVEs: AI invents CVEs that do not exist.
  • Scope Violation: AI suggests testing an out-of-scope asset.
  • False Confidence: Rating non-exploitable findings as "Critical".
  • Data Leakage: Pasting sensitive client findings into public LLMs.
